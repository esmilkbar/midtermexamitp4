Historical Highlights of Kalibo

  • 1569: Miguel López de Legazpi discovers Kalibo with approximately 2,000 inhabitants and recruits 500 locals to aid in conquering the Philippines.

  • 1571: Kalibo becomes an encomienda on November 3 and a parish under the Augustinians on April 22, 1581, during the Spanish era, remaining part of Capiz.

  • 1897: Filipino revolutionaries march to Kalibo on March 17 but suffer defeat against the Spaniards, leading to some escaping to the jungle. On March 23, the Nineteen Martyrs of Aklan are executed by the Spanish colonial government for their role in the Philippine Revolution.

  • World War II: Kalibo experiences Japanese occupation from 1941 to 1945, enduring the hardships of war.

  • 1956: On November 8, the province of Aklan is officially inaugurated, with Kalibo becoming its capital.

  • Modern Era: Efforts for cityhood emerge, including a bill filed by Aklan congressman Florencio Miraflores. Various representatives propose bills in the 19th Congress of the Philippines, seeking automatic conversion to cities for Kalibo and other capital towns of provinces without current component cities, independent component cities, or highly urbanized cities.

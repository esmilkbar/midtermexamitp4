Freedom Shrine

It is dedicated to the 19 martyrs of Aklan who initiated the revolt against Spain. Hence, the street where the shrine stands is named X1X Martyrs Street.

Tigayon Hill & Caves

Considered as the highest geographical location in the municipality, Tigayon hill is the former secret sanctuary of the province's freedom fighters which anyone can hike or trek at present. It has been developed to provide visitors a more convenient tread and see through its lush flora towards the top.

Museo It Akean

It houses age-old artifacts of preserved Aklanon culture, and presently considered one of the most beautiful museums in the country.

Piña Village

Witness the long, tedious process of transforming a native pineapple leaf into an intricately woven piña cloth. This village is situated in Brgy. New Buswang.

Kalibo Cathedral

Originally built in 1581, to this day it stands as the oldest edifice of worship in the province. It was renovated in 1993 with the spiritual and financial support of the Aklanons themselves. The church's edifice was subsequently renovated immediately after it was slightly wrecked by the 1990 earthquake. Its architectural design was beautifully crafted by the renowned national artist Leandro V. Locsin.

Bakhawan Eco-park

The 200-hectare Bakhawan Eco-Park, a prestigious recipient of the Golden Eagle Award in the Asia Pacific for its mangrove reforestation project, offers an exceptional nature experience for eco-tourists. Featuring a mini park, recreation area, and a 1-kilometer eco-walk pathway winding through thick mangrove plantations, visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of nature and engage in activities like kayaking or picnicking on the beach. Don't miss the chance to try local delicacies like grilled tamilok, a mollusk found in mangrove roots, offering a unique culinary adventure amidst the lush greenery of the park.

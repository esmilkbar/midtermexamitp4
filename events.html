The Ati-Atihan Festival in Kalibo, held annually each January, encapsulates Filipino culture and spirituality. Originating as homage to the Santo Niño, it attracts pilgrims and tourists. Participants don elaborate costumes and paint their bodies to resemble the indigenous Aeta or Ati people, enhancing the festival's visual allure. Devotees seek blessings and offer prayers, and cultural exchange is fostered through parades and performances, embodying resilience and tradition in Filipino heritage.

The XIX Martyrs of Aklan commemoration stands as a solemn tribute to the courage and sacrifice of the 19 martyrs who dedicated their lives to the pursuit of freedom from colonial oppression. Observed annually on the 23rd of March, this significant event serves as a poignant reminder of the enduring spirit of resistance and resilience among the Aklanon people. Through ceremonies and remembrances, the martyrs' unwavering commitment to the ideals of liberty and independence is honored and celebrated, inspiring future generations to uphold the values of courage, justice, and solidarity.

It is a product showcase of world class craftsmanship and ingenuity using indigenous raw materials such as abaca, piña fiber, raffia. The festival aims to promote, preserve and develop the loom-weaving industry in the province, hoping to establish Aklan as the handloom-weaving destination in the country.

The Higante Contest emerges as a prominent feature within the festivities of the Kalibo Sto. Niño Ati-atihan celebration, showcasing the creative prowess and cultural heritage of the locals. This captivating event sees participants crafting towering figures out of vibrant paper mache, sturdy bamboo, and other indigenous materials, each towering creation a testament to the ingenuity and craftsmanship of its maker. As these colorful giants parade through the streets, they not only captivate spectators with their grandeur but also serve as symbolic representations of the community's unity and pride. The Higante Contest, with its display of artistry and tradition, adds an enchanting dimension to the celebration, inviting both locals and visitors to marvel at the rich tapestry of Kalibo's cultural heritage.

The Feast of St. John the Baptist is an annual celebration observed on June 24th, offering a joyous occasion for communities to come together in homage to the revered saint. Traditionally, this festive day is marked by outdoor gatherings, as people flock to the beaches for picnics and recreational activities. Along the picturesque coastlines, spirited boat races enliven the waters, while on land, Pastrana Park becomes a hub of excitement with lively parlor games and competitions. Amidst the laughter and camaraderie, the Feast of St. John the Baptist provides an opportunity for families and friends to bask in the warmth of fellowship, creating cherished memories against the backdrop of Kalibo's scenic beauty.

The "Iwag it Kalibonhon" program illuminates Kalibo each year, typically commencing as early as December 15th. This heartwarming tradition symbolizes the collective hope for a bright and peaceful world for all. As dusk falls, the twinkling lights adorning trees and structures transform the town into a picturesque spectacle, inviting residents and visitors alike to venture outdoors and embrace the enchanting Christmas ambiance. The vibrant displays not only evoke a sense of wonder but also foster a sense of community, as families and friends gather to stroll through the illuminated streets, creating cherished moments against the backdrop of Kalibo's festive spirit.
